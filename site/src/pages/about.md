Council Discourse makes New York City Council meetings easier to navigate. Long public hearings are broken into chapters, each with a short summary, a speaker-attributed transcript, and video that jumps to the moment a chapter or line begins. The aim is practical access: a way to scan, cite, and watch hours of proceedings without sitting through the whole recording.

Everything here is built from public records. Meeting schedules, agendas, and related documents come from the Council's Legistar system, and the video comes from the Council's public video archive. Council Discourse re-hosts a copy of each meeting's video so that chapter and transcript timestamps can seek to exact moments.

The transcripts, chapter divisions, summaries, and speaker labels are generated by automated speech recognition and language models. They are useful for finding and following what was said, but they are not official and can contain errors, including misheard names, misattributed speakers, and wrong wording. When precision matters, treat the meeting video as the authoritative source.

Council Discourse is an independent project. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or an official product of the New York City Council. It is also not affiliated with citymeetings.nyc, the project that inspired this one. citymeetings.nyc set the standard for chaptered, speaker-attributed access to Council meetings; it stopped publishing new meetings in early 2026, and Council Discourse is a separate effort to keep that kind of access available.
